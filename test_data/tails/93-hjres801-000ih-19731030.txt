JOINT RESOLUTION
Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of the United States
relative to force arid effect of treaties.
1	Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives
2	of the United States of America in Congress assembled (two-
3	thirds of each House concurring therein), That the following
4	article is proposed as an amendment to the Constitution of
5	the United States, which shall he valid to all intents and pur-
6	poses as a part of the Constitution only if ratified by the legis-
7	fixtures of three-fourths of the several States within seven
8	years from the date of its submission by the Congress:
“Article
2	“Section 1. A provision of a treaty which denies or
3	abridges any right enumerated in this Constitution shall not ,
4- be of any force or effect.
5	“Sec. 2. No treaty shall authorize or permit any foreign
6	power or any international organization to supervise, control,
7	or adjudicate lights of citizens of the United States within
8	the United States enumerated in this Constitution or any
9	other matter essentially within the domestic jurisdiction of
10	the United States.
11	“Seo. 3. A treaty shall become effective as internal law
12	in the United States only through the enactment of appropri-
13	ate legislation by the Congress.
14	“Sec. 4. All executive or other agreements, between
15	the President or any international organization, foreign
16	power, or official thereof shall be made only in the manner
17	and to the extent to be prescribed by law. Such agreements
18	shall be subject to the limitations imposed on treaties, or
19	the making of treaties, by this article.	-
20	“Sec. 5. The Congress shall have power to enforce this
21	article by appropriate legislation.
22	“Sec. 6. This article shall be inoperative unless it shall
22
23	have been ratified as an amendment to the Constitution by
24	the legislatures of three-fourths of the several States within
25	seven years from the date of its submission.”.
