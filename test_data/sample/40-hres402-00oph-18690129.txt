Mr. Boutwell, from the Committee on the Judiciary, reported
the following joint resolution; which was amended so as to
read as follows:
JOINT RESOLUTION
Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of the United States.
1	Be it re^Aved by the denote and House of Representa-
2	fives of the United States of Am er in in Congress assembled,
3	(two-thirds of both houses concurring,) That the following
4	article be proposed to the legislatures of the several States as
5	an amendment to the Constitution of the United States, which,
6	when ratified by three-fourths of said legislatures, shall be held
7	as part of said Constitution, namely:
8	ARTICLE —.
9	Sec.	1. The right of any citizen of the United States	to
10	vote shall	not be denied	or abridged by the United States	or
11	any State	by reason of	race, color, or previous condition	of
12	slavery of	any citizen or	class of citizens of the United States.
2
13	Sec. 2. The Congress shall have power to enforce by
14	appropriate legislation the provisions of this article.
Mr. BINGHAM offered the following amendment to the proposi-
tion as pending: Strike out all of article one and insert as
follows:
1	Sec. 1. No State shall make or enforce any law which
2	shall abridge or deny to any male citizen of the United States
3	of sound mind and twenty-one years of age or upwards the
4	equal exercise (subject to such registration laws as the State
5	may enact) of the elective franchise at all elections in the
6	State wherein he shall have actually resided for a period of
7	one year next preceding such election, except such of said
8	citizens as shall engage in rebellion or insurrection, or who
9	may have been, or shall be, duly convicted of treason or other
10	infamous crime.
Mr. SHELLABARGER moves the following amendment to
