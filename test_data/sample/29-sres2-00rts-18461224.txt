IN THE SENATE OF THE UNITED STATES.
December 22, 1846.
Agreeably to notice, Mr. Semple asked and obtained leave to bring in the fol-
lowing joint resolution; which was read twice, and ordered to lie on the
table.
December 24,1846.
Referred to the Committee on the Judiciary, and ordered to be printed.
JOINT RESOLUTION
Proposing an amendment to the constitution of the United States, in
relation to the judicial power.
1	Revoked by the Senate and House of Representatives of
2	the United Slates of America in Congress assembled, (two
3	thirds of both Houses concurring,) That the following amend-
4	ment, when ratified in the manner provided for in the fifth ar-
5	tide, shall be added to and form a part of the constitution of the
6	United States. That is to say :
7	Nothing in the constitution, or in any amendment which
8	has been made thereto, shall be construed to authorize or em-
9	power the judicial department of the government of the United
10	States to declare any act of Congress, or of any State legislature,
fl to be void, on the ground that such act is contrary to the consti-
12	tution of the United States, or contrary to the constitution of
13	any particular State; but such power shall be held to have
14	been reserved to the States and the people in their sovereign ca-
15	pacity.
